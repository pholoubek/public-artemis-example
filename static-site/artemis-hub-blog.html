Supreme Court Expands Concealed Carry Rights Nationwide

Landmark 6-3 decision strikes down restrictive state licensing laws, affirming constitutional carry principles

By Marcus Thompson, Legal Affairs Editor Published 6:30 AM ET
Supreme Court Building & Constitution
The Supreme Court building where justices delivered the historic ruling on Second Amendment rights.

In a landmark decision that will reshape gun laws across America, the Supreme Court today ruled 6-3 that states cannot impose arbitrary restrictions on concealed carry permits, effectively establishing constitutional carry as the law of the land in all 50 states.

The ruling, which legal experts are calling the most significant Second Amendment decision since D.C. v. Heller, strikes down "may-issue" permit systems that gave local officials discretionary power to deny concealed carry applications. Justice Clarence Thomas, writing for the majority, emphasized that the right to bear arms extends beyond the home.
